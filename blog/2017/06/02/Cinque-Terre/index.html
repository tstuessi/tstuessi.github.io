Cinque Terre

June 02 2017

While we were based out of Florence, we did a bunch of traveling around northern Italy. One of my favorite places we went was Cinque Terre, a collection of five cities on the western Italian coastline. This place, while fairly packed with tourists, had absolutely stunning scenery and a fun local culture. It was an absolutely awesome day, and here is the story:

An Early Morning

The day started pretty early, as we had a 7:30 am train to catch. This meant no breakfast for us, so the day did not start out well. After a two hour ride to La Spezia, we got on another train headed to our first stop in Cinque Terre. We met a few Americans on this train (like I said, the place was touristy) and had some great conversations there, but getting off the train was where the real fun began. The first view we saw was of a beautiful coastline with mountains and cliffs, plus a sizeable beach. This was the town of Monterosso, and it was absolutely gorgeous.

As we walked through downtown, street vendors were selling locally made art and sculptures while tour groups crowded the streets. We quickly escaped the busiest streets and went up a hiking trail that faced the ocean in search of more incredible views.

Needless to say, we found them. We followed the path up the mountain to an old convent with a small chapel and cemetery, which again had stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It was peaceful and quiet up there, so I spent quite a few minutes simply watching the countryside and listening to the sounds of the ocean.

From there, we hiked back down the hill and went to a restaurant in the bustling downtown area. Frankly, I can’t imagine how anyone drives a small car through those streets let alone a supply truck, but the food was delicious and we were able to try some local cheese and charcuterie.

Once we finishedd eating, we walked around and shopped for a bit at the market. My dad ended up buying a ridiculous hat, but other than that we made it through relatively unscathed. After that, we took a ferry down the coast to the other town we were visiting, Riomaggiore.

Riomaggiore was also beautiful. The entire city was built into the hills, so the main way to get around was staircases behind buildings (much to my mom’s chagrin).

It had bustling markets and plenty of restaurants, including a cool punk bar that had frozen drinks. Naturally, we stopped to rest a bit.

However, at this point we realized that we had done as much walking as we could take, so after we finished our daquiries we left cinque terre. It was a beautiful day at a beautiful place, and that will definitely be a place we will visit again.
